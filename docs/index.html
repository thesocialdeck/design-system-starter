Public consultation: discussion paper

Overview

Have your say on the Review of the Disability Standards for Education 2005 (the Standards).

The Standards help to make sure students with disability can participate in education and training. This supports people with disability to be able to participate fully in society and have more opportunities throughout their life.

The Australian Government Department of Education, Skills and Employment, on behalf of the Minister for Education and the Attorney General, is undertaking a review of the Standards through a consultation process. The law says the Standards need to be reviewed every 5 years.

The Review will ask whether the Standards are doing their job and, if not, how they could be improved.

The Terms of Reference for the 2020 Review and more information is set out in a discussion paper. The discussion paper includes guiding questions to help you have your say. You can also access the Easy Read[link] discussion paper.

How to get involved

We want to hear from anyone with an interest in making sure students with disability can access and participate in education.

We are particularly interested in the experiences and views of students and former students with disability, their families and carers, advocates and educators. Your feedback will help us understand how well the Standards work and if changes are needed to improve them.

Our public consultations will run for three-months from 16 July 2020 to 29 September 2020.

There are a number of ways you can share your experiences, views and ideas to inform the 2020 Review. Find out more about the ways you can engage by selecting an option below.

Ways to engage

  • Make a submission

    You can make a written, audio or video submission

  • Respond to our questionnaire

    This short questionnaire will take 10-25 minutes to complete

  • Register to attend a webinar

    We will be holding four interactive webinars including a launch webinar on 23 July 2020

  • Express your interest

    You can express your interest in participating in a consultation activity, such as a focus group or online discussion
